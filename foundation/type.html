Typography


Bitter and Mulish

This typographical hierarchy combines the Bitter font, which is a serif font, and Mulish, which is a sans-serif font. These two fonts complement each other due to its contrast. Bitter is bold and sharp and used for headings, while Mulish is more calm and used for body and link text. This typographical hierarchy is arranged using a Minor Third scale with a base of 16 px.
